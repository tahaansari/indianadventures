Kanha National Park

Kanha national park trapped in a horse shoe shape valley is considered one of Asia’s finest and largest National park. Dominated by dense SAL and Bamboo forest, and the extensive Kanha meadows cover an area of 1945 sq km, ranging from an altitude of 450 to 950 meters.

Kanha is the only national park in the world inhabiting the hard ground Barasingha, along with 35 species of mammals, and over 270 species of birds and a variety of other snakes and reptiles. The captivating Forests of Kanha also provided inspiration to Rudyard Kipling for his famous perspective of “the Jungle book”.

Climatic Conditions:

  • November to March - Cold Climate
  • October and April - Moderate
  • May and June - Hot Climate
